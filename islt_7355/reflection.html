Professional reflection

I have done a lot in my life already. I have had one great love. I raised two typical, mediocre children. I have spent entirely too much time already in classes. Yet, I have repeatedly chosen careers which require regular professional development!

I tried out a lot of jobs before gaining some semblance of what I might want to do permanently. I tried food service in both a gas station and briefly a fast food restaurant. I worked customer service in a clothing store, bookstores, and gas stations. I have assembled repair kits and been a call center representative, secret shopper, tour guide, office assistant, and personal care worker!

I have spent the most time working in elementary schools, in various positions, over nearly a decade and a half. While none were quite what I wanted to do permanently, this exploration helped cement the knowledge that I wanted to work in an elementary school.

I currently serve as an exam proctor and election inspector and intend to do so until I graduate and transition to a full-time career in a school library. I eagerly await the opportunity to manage a school library and make the space my own. I want to make sure the library I work in honors the diversity of its community and reflects the diversity and wonder of the world.

Teaching philosophy

All learning is social learning. Learning occurs when the individual combines ideas, emotions, and motivations from others in her world and talks to herself using the language of others. Small group learning happens when we respond within a discourse and within those contexts that we create as a group. The small group is what mediates between the individual and the community. In communities of practice we gradually adopt the communityâ€™s ways of thinking and doing and in small ways change and add to the community through our participation. The things we think come from our interactions with others and influence and shape our individual thinking. Everything we think about, every consideration we make, is somehow influenced by others: our family, culture, and friends as well as subtle and overt influences of society.
